② · Opening Provocation
The Hook
Efficiency without relevance
is not transformation.
"If all we do is automate fragments of a low-value service, we may get efficiency without relevance. The question is not where AI fits — it is which PMO services deserve a new design."
AI can make a step faster without making the service more valuable.
A faster low-value process is still a low-value process.
The real opportunity is redesigning how the PMO creates value.

③ · Three Different Moves
Three different moves
Automate
When work is repetitive and clear
Stable, rule-based steps that should not require human attention every time. Speed and reliability are the gain.
Augment
When humans keep judgment, AI improves reach
Human still decides — but AI expands visibility, speed, or analytical depth. The judgment stays; the effort drops.
Redesign
When the service itself can change its form or value
The output, the experience, or the value delivered changes — not just how fast the old version runs.
These are not the same thing. Confusing them leads to shallow wins, inflated expectations, and weak adoption.

④ · A Simple Test
Did we make work faster — or make the service more useful?
Faster Work
Same step, less time
Faster work can be good. But if the service was low-value before, it is still low-value after.
Better Support
Same decision, better reach
Better support can be powerful. Judgment stays human. AI expands what the human can see and do.
Better Service
New form, new value
The real shift happens when the service becomes more useful to the business — not just faster internally.
Same step. Better support. Better service. The real shift is the third one.

⑥ · Example
Example: Executive Reporting
← Before
PMs rewrite status updates every week
Narratives are inconsistent across projects
Executives read updates but still need clarification
Reporting consumes time without improving decisions
After redesign →
AI drafts the first narrative from structured signals
PMs validate nuance, context, and exceptions
The PMO surfaces what actually matters earlier
Reporting becomes a decision-support service, not a formatting task
The win is not faster reporting. The win is better decision flow.

⑧ · For a Redesign to Stick
For a Redesign to Stick
Not every idea
is worth the cost.
Test your redesign against these before committing. A redesign that fails this test is probably just an automation with ambition.
Adoption is part of the design. Not something that happens later.
1
It resolves a real friction — not a hypothetical one.
2
Its value is visible to the business, not just to the PMO.
3
It has a clear owner.
4
It does not break existing flow without clear gain.
5
People trust the output enough to act on it.
