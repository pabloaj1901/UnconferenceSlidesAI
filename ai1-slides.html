AI-1 · PMO unCON 2026
What is AI? — Foundations
Systems that recognize patterns, generate outputs, and take actions — in ways that once required human judgment.
  • AI is not one thing — it's a family of approaches, each suited to different kinds of problems.
  • Not everything called "AI" actually learns. The label is used loosely — the differences are not.
  • For a PMO, the question is not "should we use AI?" — it's "which type, for which problem, right now?"

01 of 05 · AI Types
Predictive AI
Finds patterns in past data to estimate future outcomes
Uses historical data to estimate likely outcomes or risks — before they happen.
How a PMO uses it
Flags projects with rising delivery risk before they show up on the steering committee's radar.
⚠ Watch for
Bad or limited historical data produces weak or misleading predictions. Garbage in, garbage out.
When it fits
You have historical project data Patterns repeat across projects Early warning matters more than explanation Volume too high to monitor manually
AI-1 · PMO unCON 2026
02 of 05 · AI Types
Generative AI
Creates new content from instructions
AI that generates text, summaries, or drafts from natural language instructions.
How a PMO uses it
Drafts status reports, meeting summaries, and stakeholder communications from raw notes.
⚠ Watch for
Fluent output can still be wrong or overconfident. Always review before sending.
When it fits
Unstructured input needs structured output Content creation is time-consuming First draft is the bottleneck Multiple sources need synthesis
AI-1 · PMO unCON 2026
03 of 05 · AI Types
Conversational AI
Responds through natural dialogue
AI designed to respond through chat or voice in a back-and-forth exchange.
How a PMO uses it
A project assistant for questions, updates, and on-demand guidance — in plain language.
⚠ Watch for
Good dialogue can hide shallow understanding. Always verify critical answers.
When it fits
Information exists but is hard to navigate Repetitive questions consume team time Users need guidance, not just data 24/7 availability matters
AI-1 · PMO unCON 2026
04 of 05 · AI Types
Agentic AI
Acts autonomously across multiple steps
AI that doesn't wait to be asked. It plans, acts, and coordinates across systems — autonomously.
How a PMO uses it
Gathers updates, consolidates, flags risks, and routes actions — without a human orchestrating each step.
⚠ Watch for
Weak controls create errors nobody intended. The more autonomous the system, the more important the guardrails.
When it fits
Multi-step workflows run on schedule Coordination spans multiple systems Human orchestration is the bottleneck Context must carry across steps
AI-1 · PMO unCON 2026
05 of 05 · AI Types
RPA
Robotic Process Automation
Follows fixed rules across systems
Software automation that repeats structured tasks based on fixed rules. No learning involved.
How a PMO uses it
Moves data between systems and assembles standard reports automatically, same steps every time.
⚠ Watch for
Process changes break the automation immediately. It only knows one path — change a step and it fails.
When it fits
Fixed rules, no exceptions Structured data across stable systems High-volume, low-judgment tasks Process is stable and well-documented

AI is not the strategy.
It is an enabler.
The biggest risk is not moving too slowly — it's moving noisily, without value logic. Use these 5 criteria to evaluate your top opportunity.
Two-track logic
QUICK WIN
Visible result in 30–60 days
Builds trust with leadership
Connects to a long-term goal
STRATEGIC FOUNDATION
Slow and unglamorous
But structurally necessary
